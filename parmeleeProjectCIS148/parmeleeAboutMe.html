About Me

I was very lucky to grow up in Southern California. During the day, I played wiffle ball and rode scooters in my backyard. At night, I would check on my Neopets and try to beat the hardest bosses in Final Fantasy. Our neighborhood was perfect to me. Everyone knew each other and we hung out as a crowd. Yearly, we would have a table tennis tournament. It was perfect.

After graduating from San Clemente High School (and beating Dana Hills in tennis my senior year, in your face Dolphins!), I went to USC. But not that USC. University of South Carolina. It was a wild ride, balancing school and how to be an elementary school teacher with football games and meeting new people. Before my senior year, I embarked on a road trip with my friend, Haley. It was officially a journey for our senior thesis, but it became a journey that would change our lives forever.

The road trip took us from South Carolina, along the southern coast of the United States, to California. Along the way, we used Couchsurfing, a free way to stay in and explore a city. We also camped in National and State Parks, stayed with family, and occasionally had to get a hotel. After meeting up with my family in San Clemente, we went up the west coast.

It was at this point where Haley and I fell in love. Again, my life was picturesque. It was also at this point where we got a call from our college dorm that they could reimburse us for some of the gas! We continued from Seattle across the northern highways to Detroit. From there, we took a little detour up to Toronto and back through Niagara Falls. Our journey ended and we defended our thesis in front of everyone who supported us throughout.

This trip propelled our lives forward. Haley used the last semester of her senior year to intern in Washington D.C. and I found a job teaching 4th grade just outside the city limits in Maryland. Our seven years in D.C. included the snowiest Blizzard in the history of D.C., getting married, and adopting two beautiful and mischievous cats.

Since moving to California, I have rediscovered myself. I did not know I could be as happy as I am now that I live in Los Angeles. I hope to one day give back to L.A. in a way that is proportional. Since deciding to change careers, I have explored different ways to do that. I am hoping to start by sharing my love of L.A. with people who visit, then eventually working for the county or city in some capacity.
